Project deep dive

Smart Audio Transcript Player

A Figma prototype for a listening-audio feature that helps language learners replay specific words, follow a synchronized transcript, and hear pronunciation in selected accents.

Project summary

This prototype began from a workplace request at Berlitz. A supervisor shared that students were having trouble replaying individual words in listening-audio materials, so I explored how the audio player could make word-level replay easier.

I designed the Figma prototype as a product reference for the LMS partner working with Berlitz. An LMS, or Learning Management System, is software used to deliver courses and learning activities. The goal was to show how Berlitz hoped the listening section in Parta.io, the LMS platform Berlitz was using with a partner company, could support transcript display, pronunciation replay, accent selection, and two-way navigation between audio and transcript.

Product context

Audience
Language learners using digital listening materials inside Berlitz's online learning environment.
Core problem
Students could not easily replay one missed word without scrubbing through the full listening audio.
Design medium
Figma prototype and demo video used to communicate desired listening-audio functionality to an LMS partner.
My contribution
Feature concept, Figma prototype, interaction flow, transcript/audio behavior, and stakeholder-facing demo explanation.

Prototype feature set

The demo video and transcript show a listening player that makes audio more searchable, replayable, and useful for pronunciation-focused language learning.

  1. Transcript button Adds a transcript icon to the audio bar so learners can open the full transcript while listening.
  2. Scrollable transcript Keeps long transcripts usable inside a popup panel without covering the whole learning page.
  3. Accent selector Lets learners choose which pronunciation accents, such as American and British, should be available.
  4. Selected-state feedback Shows a check mark after an accent is selected, so learners know which options are active.
  5. Hover-to-reveal audio Highlights a transcript word on hover and reveals a sound icon beside the word.
  6. Word-level replay Lets learners click a word in the transcript to hear that word's pronunciation instead of replaying the full audio.
  7. Accent-specific playback Plays the selected accent pronunciations in sequence, and highlights the accent currently being played.
  8. Vocabulary and phrase support Extends the same sound-icon behavior to vocabulary and phrase lists, with the icon visible all the time.
  9. Audio-to-transcript sync Marks the word currently being read in the transcript so learners do not have to search manually.
  10. Transcript-to-audio seeking Lets learners click a transcript word to move the audio bar to that word's timestamp.

Interaction flow

The prototype starts from a familiar audio bar and adds a transcript icon. When learners open the transcript, they can scroll through the text, choose available accents, and interact directly with words.

  • Opening the transcript keeps listening, reading, and replay in one place.
  • Hover states make interactive words discoverable without adding constant visual noise.
  • Accent menus give learners control over which pronunciation models they want to hear.
  • Two-way sync lets learners move from audio to transcript or from transcript back to audio.

Learning rationale

The design supports listening comprehension by reducing the friction between noticing a missed word and hearing it again. It also supports pronunciation awareness by letting learners compare accent models.

  • Word-level replay supports focused listening instead of broad trial-and-error rewinding.
  • Synchronized highlighting helps learners connect sound, spelling, and transcript context.
  • Accent selection makes pronunciation support more flexible for different learning goals.

Design decisions

The prototype uses small interaction details to make the feature understandable to learners and feasible for product stakeholders to review.

Keep the player familiar

The transcript icon is added to the existing audio bar so the new feature feels like an extension of the current listening workflow.

Prevent text blocking

The transcript popup is scrollable, and the accent control is positioned so it does not cover the transcript text.

Make states visible

Hover highlights, sound icons, green checks, and active accent highlights show what can be clicked and what is currently playing.

Design for implementation

The Figma prototype translates a learner pain point into concrete behaviors the LMS partner could inspect and build from.

Portfolio takeaway

Smart Audio Transcript Player shows my ability to turn a learner complaint into a concrete product feature, prototype the interaction details, and communicate language-learning requirements to platform stakeholders.

  • Connected audio, transcript, pronunciation, and navigation into one learner workflow.
  • Created a Figma demo to help an LMS partner understand the desired functionality for Parta.io.
